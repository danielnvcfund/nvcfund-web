About Standby Letters of Credit

A Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC) is a guarantee from a bank on behalf of a client that a payment will be made to a beneficiary if certain conditions are not met. Unlike traditional Letters of Credit, SBLCs are only drawn upon in case of default.

Key Information
  • SBLCs are transmitted via SWIFT MT760 message from the issuing bank to the advising bank.
  • Processing typically takes 1-3 business days after the message is sent.
  • The SBLC remains in effect until its expiry date or until a drawing is made against it.
  • If the issuing party defaults on their obligation, the beneficiary can draw on the SBLC by presenting documentation to the bank.

For more information on the status of your Letter of Credit, please contact our customer support.
