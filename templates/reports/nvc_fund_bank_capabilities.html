I. INSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATION

A. Legal Framework

NVC Fund Bank operates under a comprehensive legal framework established through multiple jurisdictional recognitions:

  • Banking Charter: Established as an "Expressed Trust Bank" under the African Union Treaty and the African Finance Regulatory Authority (AFRA)
  • Supranational Status: Operates under the sovereign decree establishing the African Central Bank (ACB) and Article XIV 1(e) of the ECO-6 Treaty
  • Corporate Structure: Formally organized with a board of directors and defined governance structure
  • Historical Lineage: Successor to financial services entities dating back to 1982, formally consolidated in 2000

B. Organizational History

Founded on October 25, 2000, NVC Fund Holding Trust emerged as the successor to several financial entities operated by Frank Ekejija since 1982, including International Business Services, The Unique Group, Strategic Financial Group, Samson, Gaskin, and Pettit, and Network Venture Capital Fund.

The pivotal development in the bank's history came between 2005-2007 through the acquisition of 22 Wyoming mineral resources claims, one of which was appraised at $1.3 trillion in gold, silver, platinum, and rhodium reserves. This asset base formed the foundation for the bank's substantial financial capacity.

C. Institutional Recognition

NVC Fund Bank has established formal recognition across multiple premier financial systems:

  • U.S. Treasury Recognition: Established TreasuryDirect account with the U.S. Department of the Treasury, Bureau of Public Debt (documented 2009-2011)
  • Global Securities Registration: Formal ISIN/CUSIP registration (US62944AAA43) with CUSIP Global Services
  • Financial Market Integration: Bloomberg listing with assigned ticker 3387420Z US (established September 2009)
  • Securities Exchange Commission: SEC Form 6 registration of NVC trust shares as Unit Investment Trust with CUSIP #67074B105 (March 27, 2009)
