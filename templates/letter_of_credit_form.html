What is a Standby Letter of Credit?

A Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC) is a legal document that guarantees a bank's commitment of payment to a seller in case the buyer defaults on the agreement. The bank will issue this document on behalf of a customer and hold it as a guarantee.

Key Features

  • Provides security for international and domestic trade
  • Issued using the international SWIFT MT760 message format
  • Recognized by financial institutions worldwide
  • Can be customized with specific terms and conditions
  • Typically valid for up to 1 year, renewable if needed
