About SWIFT Transfers

The SWIFT network connects over 11,000 financial institutions worldwide, allowing secure international money transfers.

There are two main types of SWIFT transfer messages:

  • MT103 - Used for customer credit transfers (personal or business)
  • MT202 - Used for financial institution transfers (bank-to-bank)

About SWIFT Messaging

SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication) is a global member-owned cooperative that provides secure financial messaging services. Banks use SWIFT to securely transmit information and instructions through a standardized system of codes.

Key Information
  • The MT103 message is the most common SWIFT message for cross-border payments, used for individual or business transfers.
  • The MT202 message is used for financial institution transfers (bank-to-bank).
  • Processing times vary depending on the receiving institution's policies and procedures.
  • Status updates are typically available within 24-48 hours after a transfer is initiated.
